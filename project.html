Projects

With the development of wearable sensing device(WSD), the advancement of Micro-Electro-Mechanical System (MEMS) and Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) technology, inertial sensors have become indispensable in the field of motion analysis and motion recognition Status. In recent years, many researchers have conducted studies on foot dominance movements, because the changes in the feet and legs of athletes during exercise are very important. In traditional research, most researchers use an action recognition method based on computer vision, and this method has been relatively mature. However, this method requires high light, cannot be performed in a dark environment, and the video-based motion recognition algorithm is computationally expensive, and the equipment is expensive. The WSD offers the advantage of dynamic monitoring of multiple players in indoor and outdoor environments.

Development in wireless communication and sensing is pushing the operating frequencies of dielectric devices - relevant to filters, antennas, resonators and phase shifters - from radio frequency (RF) to the microwave (MW) band and the terahertz (THz) band. Preferred dielectric properties at frequencies above RF are reasonably high dielectric permittivity, high quality factor (low loss) and low temperature coefficient . As dielectric permittivity and loss of a material is frequency dependent, it is necessary to characterize dielectric properties at MW and THz bands for development of high frequency applications.
